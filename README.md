**`Abstract`**

This thesis explores the interplay between security and usability within the scope of different authentication methods. The main focus is the comparison between the innovative passkey-based authentication method, using the emerging FIDO2 WebAuthn protocol for passwordless passkey-based authentication compared to traditional authentication methods in password- and passphrase-based authentication. By using a privacy calculus framework, the focus revolves around user interactions with different authentication scenarios, providing insights about the preferences and compromises users are inclined towards in the pursuit of secure yet usable authentication processes. The analysis, with both quantitative and qualitative insights, reveals that the trade-offs users are willing to make depend on both the kind of service they are using, combined with their trust and sense of control in how their data is handled. These results suggest that there is a desire for security measures that are both easy to use, combined with a heightened sense of security compared to the traditional password-based authentication method.

In addition to this, the results also suggest a positive reception towards passkey-based authentication, highlighting its potential to mitigate common vulnerabilities with passwords while also retaining an easy and simple way to authenticate the user. Despite this, some challenges towards widespread adoption of this technology are present. The main challenge identified is the necessity for users to understand the benefits of passkeys in order to be willing to replace other more familiar authentication methods. This understanding was achieved within the scope of this thesis; however, this can be more difficult in a broader perspective. Without knowledge of what passkeys entail and the advantages they offer, users may be reluctant to switch from familiar and comfortable patterns if they can not weigh the perceived benefits against perceived risks. Therefore, this thesis highlights the need for transparent communication strategies to lessen this gap, ensuring users are well-informed and comfortable in embracing passkey-based authentication which can be summarized as a secure and user-friendly alternative compared to traditional authentication methods.
