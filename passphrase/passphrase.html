Scenario 2

Username With Long Passphrase

Description

In this scenario, you will engage with an enhanced security login system that requires a longer "passphrase" for authentication. This method builds on the traditional username and password approach by enforcing a minimum passphrase length of 14 characters. The goal is to increase security by making passphrases harder to guess or crack, protecting users' accounts and sensitive information more effectively. In this scenario you also get the option to encrypt your data if you want to, this means that the owner of the website can not read your data. This is a trade off in terms of usability versus privacy.

Task 1: Register

Register by entering your first name, last name, secret, email and passphrase into the respective fields, these credentials do not have to be real you can make them up if you prefer that. In this case you will use a passphrase, the recommended way of doing this is to create a sentence with a mix of capital letters that is easy to remember for you. The passphrase has to be a minimum of 14 characters. Make sure that you remember the credentials after registering. Click on the "Register" button to finish registration.

Examples of passphrases: "I love ice cream!", "My Dog Is A Beatiful Dog!", "IWantToGoToNewYork!"

Task 2: Login

Enter your email and passphrase into the respective fields. Click the "Login" button to complete the authentication process.

Privacy Notice

In this scenario you have the option if you would like the data to be encrypted or not. If you decide to encrypt the data the company that owns the data can not read it. However, if you decide to encrypt it, you will not be able to recover your account if you forget the passphrase.

You can now decide if you want to encrypt the data. The researchers removes all credentials after the experiment is done.

Would you like to encrypt the data so that the owner of the website can not read it?:
Warning! Remember to write down your passphrase somewhere safe because you will not be able to recover it in the future. The owner of the data can not help you recover the passphrase because your data is encrypted and not readable by the website.
