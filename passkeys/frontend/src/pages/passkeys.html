Scenario 4

Passkeys

Description:

In this scenario, you will explore the latest approach of authentication using only a passkey. with no username or password required. A passkey is like a digital key you use to unlock your online accounts, such as email or social media, without having to remember complicated passwords. Instead of typing a password, your device (like your phone or computer) confirms it's really you and automatically logs you in. This approach enhances security by eliminating the vulnerabilities associated with password-based systems, such as phishing and brute force attacks.

Task 1:

Register with one of the presented alternatives.

iCloud Keychain: The passkey will be saved on your iCloud and will be accessible on every device with you Apple ID

Use a phone or table: You will be provided with QRCode and asked to save the passkey to your iCloud Keychain

Your Chrome profile: The passkey will be saved locally on the computer and will not be accessible on other devices

USB security key: Insert the security key into the computer, the passkey will then be saved on the security key. You have to insert the security key in the future to authenticate

Task 2:

Login using the option you previously used to create your passkey, iCloud, Phone, Chrome or USB key

You can now decide if you want the website to be able to read your information. Basically encrypting it or not. When you encrypt it, the owner of the website/company can not read your information. If you do not encrypt the data, they can.
