Scenario 3

Passkeys

Description:

In this scenario, you will explore the latest approach of authentication using only a "passkey" with no password required. A passkey is a digital key that is saved on your own device, Apple or Google account which is only accessible to you. This means that you do not have to enter an email or password in order to login. Instead of typing a password, your device (phone or computer) confirms it's really you and automatically signs you in with the passkey. This approach enhances security by eliminating the vulnerabilities associated with password-based systems, such as phishing and brute force attacks because no one can access your passkey except from you.

Task 1:

Register with one of the presented alternatives.

iCloud Keychain: The passkey will be saved on your iCloud and will be accessible on every device with your Apple ID

Use a phone or table: You will be provided with QR code and asked to save the passkey to your iCloud Keychain

Your Chrome profile: The passkey will be saved locally on your computer in the Chrome browser and will not be accessible on other devices or browsers meaning that you only use this passkey on this computer on the Chrome browser

USB security key: Insert the security key into the computer, the passkey will then be saved on the security key. You have to insert the security key in the future to authenticate. You can use this security key to login with the passkey from anywhere

Task 2:

Login using the option you previously used to create your passkey, iCloud, Phone, Chrome or USB key

Privacy Notice

The information will not be encrypted. If you loose your passkey your account can not be recovered. The researchers removes all credentials after the experiment is done.
